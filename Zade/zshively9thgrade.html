9th Grade

In 9th grade I have done many projects. Below I will tell you about a few of them in my essay.






Introduction

I try my best to ask clarifying questions when I really do not understand something. In the beginning
of 9th grade I thought everything little thing NEEDED to be perfect. Therefore, I did not like asking
for help. Now, I do not think everything needs to be perfect and I ask for help. I understand that
sometimes I just need to chill out and not let the stress get to me. For example if it is a week
before a due date then I do not freak out and just start working. I also have learned some more skills
to further my life, sometimes stuff just happen but you need to just deal with it and get back up
again. In 9th grade, I have learned that not everything needs to be perfect; this will help me with
becoming a sophomore.

How I Excelled

In Chinese, I excelled in the Rap Project. To excel means to do greatly at something. It was where I had to
use multiple phrases and words in Chinese and make it into a rap/song. I was doing well in Chinese at the time
and it was going well. I had a group I was working with to help create it. We had to create a script. After,
we made the script you would practice it and create a ‘dance’ routine. Then, we all got together and made the
final video before submitting it. I do not have the final video due to the fact it was not turned in by me,
but I have the pretest of Chinese and the post-test. You will be able to see that during the year I
have vastly increased my knowledge of the Chinese language compared to the pretest. We all worked on the>
project and that allowed the end result to be well done.

How I Struggled

I struggled in Language Art’s Project called, “Post-Apocalyptic Story Project”. Mainly because I wrote
too much and did not want to delete any of it. I had to try hard to delete bits out and figuring out what
to keep in the story. First, you created your character and your group decides on where to locate yourselves.
Then, we dove head first into shaping and creating their stories. My character was a cool character and was a
decent human being unless it came to Oliver. She hates that guy he gets hurt for no apparent reason and her
being of the medical background has to fix him up, though if he’s on her nerves too much or this has already
happened a lot today she will not help him. Then, in the end, you choose your favorite part and copy it in
the final draft. Then do a bunch of proof reading and page editing. In the end, I still kept it the same so
I failed to overcome said struggle. However, if you see my final here, I had done a decent enough job, so
when our group’s book was graded we did not do too bad. I believe we all got out of that project with a B or A-.

How I Grew

I grew the most in Engineering in a project called, “The 3D Print Project”. You would have to print an object to help
solve a problem or issue. I went with a ring holder and used a design like a hand. I found out afterward that apparently
some people before me failed to do this, while I succeeded. Granted, this project was not a walk in the park, it had many
problems. In the beginning, I failed it multiple times because I could not figure out a shape to make the rings go on.
Therefore, after much work and ideas, I used a slightly made up hand, relative to mine (ie: fingers, knuckles, etc...).
As you can see in this beginning of the idea I did not have much way to support the weight of the hand it was just too
steep of an angle. The first print did not work, for I had filleted (Made it hollow on the inside) the entire thing with
a program called Autodesk Inventor Professional. Inventor is a software is under the CAD category (computer aided drafting),
and it lets you make 3D models accurately. Anyways, I filleted it so it would not take up much filament since I still did
not really know how the filling worked. However, when I started printing it, it came out as spirals of just filament.
Turns out that you cannot fillet something then print it, because it already filleted it in a way. It uses crisscross
patterns so the build can be built up and be sturdy. To fix this issue I just deleted the fillet and just like that it
was working. I started it up and left it on to continue while I was off on break. When break was over, it was finished
and looked very cool and actually worked really well, to my own surprise. I still use it to this day. This is the outcome
of this project and I quite like it. I also, like that even though I do not have 10 fingers on it for all the places for
my rings on each hand. I also like that the outcome does not take up too much space and is still easy to use.

How I'm Ready For 10th Grade

I was like a total nerd in the beginning of the year (still kind of am), but I am a lot more chill now. I really had to rely on inquiry
because I have had to ask for help a lot this year. I am ready enough for 10th grade because I will at least ask for help when I need
it and know that not everything has to be perfect. For example, in 1st –beginning 9th grade year (first quarter), I had gotten all A’s
and B’s. Now, that is totally fine, but I worked excessively hard on my homework and could even say the first draft looked like a final.
I just spent too much work on something that was only used to test me or to just get an idea out. Now, near the end of 9th grade, I can say
I most certainly do not do this anymore; I just do not have the time anymore to do that anymore. Therefore, I just have to change it, therefore
I did. I say ready enough because no one is ever completely ready but I am ready enough to move forward in my life. In 10th grade, I hope I will
figure out what I want to do career wise.
