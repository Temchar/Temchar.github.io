Frustration in Fitness

Martin Luther King Jr., a civil rights activist,
once said,” If you can’t fly, then run, If you can’t run, then walk, If you can’t walk, then crawl,
But whatever you do, You have to keep moving forward.” In this I used persistence when I
felt like giving up but didn’t, I kept moving forward like Martin Luther King Jr. did. He tried and
tried to get a future where all people would be treated equally and would get
the same rights, and he was successful by preserving. Persistence is when you feel as though giving
up but instead you keep going. This year I have improved in the
quality of persistence, which has helped me become more skilled in cardiovascular endurance.

In this task I have grown in persistence, which has helped me become better at
cardiovascular endurance. Cardiovascular endurance is jogging, walking, and skipping, it’s basically
stamina. For this task we started off continuously jogging for gym. As it got later in the year the time we
had to continuously jog increased by half a minute every two weeks, which made it get harder and harder till
it reached the point where I was like “HOW?!?” or “How can you go any longer?!?”This was because I’m not someone
who’s good at continuously working out. So this is where persistence comes in, when you feel like giving up and
feel as though it’s impossible but keep going. When I got to three minutes for the continuous jog I thought to myself:
“HOW?!?” Because I felt I reached my limit, though right now we are on five minutes and counting and I still have
not reached my limit. But through persistence I have made it through five and a half minutes of continuously jogging.
The time is continuously increasing by 30 seconds every 2 weeks. Yes, it took time, effort, and practice but I made it!
A helpful tip that helped me make it was controlled breathing. Controlled Breathing was while you were jogging you
would breathe in through your nose hold it for a few seconds and then exhale it through your mouth. This is supposed
to help you keep oxygen in you so you don’t collapse from lack of oxygen. This helped a lot as the times of continuously
jogging got longer. After Jogging we would do exercises or games. To keep track of our data we had Fitness charts.
In the fitness charts we‘d keep the date played, RPE, and heartrate throughout the activity. RPE is a scale to rate
ratio for how hard you worked. For example: 1 would be like, “oh my god, I could do this in my sleep”, and 10 would
be like “HOW CAN YOU DO THIS?!? IT’S SOO HARD!” RPE stands for Rate of Perceived Exertion. As you can see from the
beginning of the Fitness chart, we did a walk/jog, the walk/jog dated as 10/7/16 was really hard because I personally
am not super active, and so I had a harder start. But as time passes we can see a slight difference in the newer chart,
the walk/jog dated as 2/23/17, and the numbers for RPE goes down a bit and the heart rate becomes more of a regular number.
But after Christmas break the activities gets harder, because Mrs. Curran increased the complexity of the game, so as we get
better at cardiovascular endurance we don’t stop getting a work out.

Since I have grown at the quality persistence, I have improved to become better at
cardiovascular endurance. In this portfolio you can see how I felt like giving up but instead I
practiced with time and effort to become better at cardiovascular endurance. It is important I
improved in this quality because this has helped me understand that if you do something enough and
push yourself, you can do it no matter how hard it may be.

Questioning Myself

Albert Einstein, a scientist, once said, “The important thing is to not stop questioning.”
In these DBQ's I had to use inquiry to keep on questioning myself. Inquiry is when you are asking questions
and/or are trying to get an answer. We had to use our previous knowledge and effectively pull evidence from
the documents. Like Albert, I always have to keep questioning myself to make sure I have everything I need.
This year, I have improved in the quality of inquiry, which has helped me to become more skilled at writing skills
in World History.

In this activity, I grown in inquiry, which has helped me become more skilled at questioning
my work from DBQ’s. In World History we answer DBQ’s. DBQ means Document Based Question. These questions
are meant to tie into the subject you are working on at that time. DBQ’s require a claim, effective use of
your document, prior knowledge, evidence, how much you have on the page, and little to no writing errors.
In one of my older DBQ’s I forgot to pull evidence from the documents for the difference. In this case I
forgot to question if I have all the requirements. In this DBQ we were to compare similarities and differences
between the ancient Greek Olympics to modern day Olympics. The ancient and modern Olympics are sporting events
that require skill. For example in the older Olympics they would do Chariot races but instead we do horse races.
In one of my newer DBQ’s I have all of the requirements. I wrote a lot and questioned enough that I had done
everything needed to get full points. This one talks about the Magna Carta compared to the bill of rights. The
Magna Carta is a list of laws the president can’t deprive of us, and The Bill of Rights is a list that has been
written for individual purposes and limits the government, that he/she can’t pass. The Magna Carta has less
specific laws than the Bill of Rights. But the Manga Carta was made before the Bill of Rights, in fact, the
Magna Carta was the first act of rights to civilians.

Since I have inproved in the quality of inquiry, I am better able to question myself. It
is important you enhance this quality because it helps you make something better than it already was.
For example if you got feedback on something, say art, you would most likely do better than if you didn't
get feedback.
