Patience with Math

Over this semester, I have overcome a challenge brought with learning math called patience; this has come after many assignments of not understanding.
I have learned this from many assignments and/or projects in many classes however, I feel as though that has been excessively so for math this year. While
math has not always been the hardest subject, it definitely has become it. And yes, while I have learned from it, it doesn’t mean it’s entirely over.
I have overcome a few thing in math, yes, but I have not overcome math itself completely yet. I think that will take many years.

One of the assignments was Factoring Quadratic Functions. In math, we use a website that is supposed to help us become better at it. This website is
called IXL and while I understand its purpose, I do not always understand it. Which in itself has been a problem that has not been fixed yet. However,
IXL has always particularly difficult for me. Others have some troubles with it because one of the two participants of the action of getting the answer wrong.

The lessons and notes of Math class itself do not help much either. The notes or worksheet is more of copy down the notes fast enough rather than actually
learning what the lesson is. This makes me fall behind sometimes. The lecture does not always make sense to me so I have to find outside resources to help me
understand the lesson. Some of these include, more knowledgeable friends, YouTube, the internet, etc…

On the Reference Triangles and Reciprocal Trig Functions Worksheet, we did some lessons. Then we were taught how to find triangles sides, and angles. For triangles’
sides we used either two sides and an angle or an angle and two sides. For triangles’ angles, we used two angles or sides and an angle/ two. However, I still did not
understand how to do them, and even when I asked for help, the help I got did not help. It is not the teachers fault; I just know what type of help I necessarily needed.
It turns out I needed step by step, hands on, visual learning.

Also on the Graph Quadratic Functions in Standard Form Worksheet, I had some troubles on. I never have been great at graphing things in math. This worksheet is one that can,
and has definitely proves this statement. I still do not fully understand how to do it. Nevertheless, we get better from practice and practice I have. But, at that time,
I did not understand it and did not do great on the assignment.

Finally, I have done my best in this class and with doing so have learned from it. Patience is a necessity in life. If you are hasty and are impatient,
then in life you will be rewarded with anger, and who wants to be angry their entire life. If you have learned patience, in your most frustrating situations,
you can have a calm outlook on it and eventually it may come to you. Learning takes time, patience and acceptance. It may be awhile before you get it or you may
get it in a jiffy.
